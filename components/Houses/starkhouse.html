EDDARD STARK

Eddard "Ned" Stark is a fictional character in the 1996 fantasy novel A Game of Thrones by George R. R. Martin, and Game of Thrones, HBO's adaptation of Martin's A Song of Ice and Fire series. In the storyline, Ned is the lord of Winterfell, an ancient fortress in the North of the fictional continent of Westeros. For several millennia, the Starks were not the uncontested Kings in the North. Their primary antagonists.

Arya Stark

Arya Stark is the third child of Eddard Stark and Catelyn Stark. After narrowly escaping the persecution of House Stark by House Lannister, Arya is trained as a Faceless Man at the House of Black and White in Braavos, and uses her new skills to bring those who have wronged her family to justice

CATELYN STARK

Lady Catelyn Stark,[1][6][7] née Catelyn Tully,[8] is the wife of Eddard Stark, Lord of Winterfell and Warden of the North. Together they have five children: Robb, Sansa, Arya, Bran, and Rickon. Catelyn is known as Cat to close family and friends.Starks gradually defeated rival kings.

Brandon Stark

Brandon Stark, commonly called Bran, is the fourth child and second son of Eddard and Catelyn Stark. Jon Snow

Jon Snow, born Aegon Targaryen, is the son of Lyanna Stark and Rhaegar Targaryen, the late Prince of Dragonstone. After successfully capturing a wight and presenting it to the Lannisters as proof that the Army of the Dead are real, Jon pledges himself and his army to Daenerys Targaryen

Sansa Stark

Sansa Stark is the eldest daughter of Eddard Stark of Winterfell and his wife Catelyn. She initially starts off with a very naive view of the world, but as time goes on she becomes a more hardened and learned individual
